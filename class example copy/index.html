Evolution of the iPhones.

iPhone 1 | 2007

The original iPhone introduced a revolutionary touch-based interface and combined features of a phone, iPod, and internet communicator. It had a 3.5-inch display, a 2-megapixel camera, and ran on iOS.

iPhone 3, 3GS | 2008-2009

The iPhone 3G introduced support for 3G data networks, making internet browsing faster. It also brought GPS capabilities, App Store access, and the iPhone OS 2.0
with third-party app support.

The iPhone 3GS improved performance with a faster processor, more RAM, and a 3-megapixel camera capable of recording video. It also introduced voice control and expanded storage options.

iPhone 4, 4S | 2010-2011

The iPhone 4 featured a major redesign with a glass and stainless steel body, a high-resolution Retina display, and a front-facing camera for FaceTime video calls. It introduced iOS 4 with multitasking support.

The iPhone 4S introduced Siri, the intelligent virtual assistant, and improved camera capabilities with an 8-megapixel sensor and 1080p video recording. It also offered better performance and introduced iOS 5.

iPhone 5, 5C, 5S | 2012-2013

The iPhone 5 featured a taller 4-inch display, a thinner profile, and a faster A6 processor. It introduced a new Lightning connector, improved camera performance, and iOS 6 with Apple Maps.

The iPhone 5S introduced Touch ID, a fingerprint scanner embedded in the home button. It also had an improved camera and introduced the 64-bit A7 chip. The iPhone 5C was a more affordable model with a plastic shell and multiple color options.

iPhone 6, 6 Plus, 6S, 6S Plus | 2014-2015

The iPhone 6 and 6 Plus featured larger screens at 4.7 inches and 5.5 inches, respectively. They introduced a rounded design, NFC for Apple Pay, improved cameras, and the A8 chip. They also launched with iOS 8.

The iPhone 6S and 6S Plus introduced 3D Touch, a pressure-sensitive display technology. They featured improved cameras, a faster A9 chip, and a new
rose gold color option. They launched with iOS 9.

iPhone 7, 7 Plus | 2016

The iPhone 7 and 7 Plus removed the headphone jack
and introduced water resistance. They featured a new A10
Fusion chip, improved cameras with optical zoom on the 7 Plus, and launched with iOS 10.

iPhone 8, 8 Plus | 2017

The iPhone 8 and 8 Plus featured glass backs for wireless charging, introduced the A11 Bionic chip, and improved the cameras. They launched with iOS 11.

iPhone X, XR, XS, XS Max | 2018

The iPhone X marked the 10th anniversary of the iPhone and introduced a bezel-less design with a notch. It featured Face ID for secure facial recognition, an OLED display, the A11 Bionic chip, and launched with iOS 11.

The iPhone XS and XS Max offered improvements over the iPhone X, including an upgraded A12 Bionic chip, improved cameras, and faster Face ID. The iPhone XR featured a lower-cost LCD display and a single rear camera.

iPhone 11, 11 Pro, 11 Pro
Max | 2019

The iPhone 11 series introduced the A13 Bionic chip,
improved cameras with Night Mode, and a new
ultra-wide-angle lens. The iPhone 11 Pro and 11 Pro Max
had triple-camera systems, while the iPhone 11 had a dual-camera setup.

iPhone 12 Mini, 12,
12 Pro, 12 Pro Max | 2020

The iPhone 12 series featured a flat-edge design
reminiscent of the iPhone 4. They introduced 5G
support, the A14 Bionic chip, improved cameras,
and the MagSafe magnetic accessory system. The iPhone 12 mini
was a smaller variant.

iPhone 13 Mini, 13,
13 Pro, 13 Pro Max
| 2021

The iPhone 13 series featured smaller notches,
improved cameras with sensor-shift optical image
stabilization, the A15 Bionic chip, and ProMotion
displays with a 120Hz refresh rate. The iPhone 13 Pro models offered ProMotion
Super Retina XDR displays and advanced photography capabilities.

iPhone 14, 14 Plus, 14
Pro, 14 Pro Max | 2022

The larger 6.7-inch screen on the iPhone 14 Plus is the
same size as the screen on the iPhone 14 Pro Max. The
iPhone 14 and iPhone 14 Plus both feature longer
battery lives, better cameras and are switching to a tray
without a SIM card.

The iPhone 14 series also introduces a crash detection
feature that detects when you're in an accident and an
emergency satellite SOS system that enables users to connect to satellites and inform emergency personnel.
